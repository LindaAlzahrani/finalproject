What Is Video Games?

A video game or computer game is an electronic game that involves interacting with a user interface or input device (like a joystick, controller, keyboard, or motion sensor) to produce visual feedback on a screen, such as a TV, computer monitor, handheld device, or VR headset. Most modern games are audiovisual, with sound delivered through speakers or headphones, and some also offer sensory feedback, like haptic technology. Additionally, some games support microphone and webcam inputs for chatting and livestreaming.
